This section isolates one question in the simplified proof attempt:

\subsection*{Question}
Let
\[
\Phi(q):=\Gamma(q)(1-q)\zeta(q),
\qquad
\mathcal{B}:=\{q\in\C:0<\RePart(q)<1,\ \Phi(q)=0\}.
\]
The central open step is Target~B from \cref{thm:target-uniqueness}:
\[
q\in\mathcal{B}\Longrightarrow \RePart(q)=\tfrac12.
\]

\subsection*{Why this is decisive}
With Target~A (\cref{thm:target-correspondence}), the statement above excludes all off-line nontrivial zeros.
That implication is exactly the conditional RH reduction in \cref{prop:rh-reduction-main-spine}.
