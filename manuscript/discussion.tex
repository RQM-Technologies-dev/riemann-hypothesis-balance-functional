\paragraph{Main simplification decision.}
The manuscript now fixes one canonical main-text object:
\[
\Phi(q):=\Gamma(q)(1-q)\zeta(q).
\]
Balance is defined directly by $\Phi(q)=0$.
This is an intentional simplification for mathematical audit.

\paragraph{What this revision changes.}
The main flow is now:
(1) define $\Phi$,
(2) state zero--balance correspondence target,
(3) state one critical-line exclusion target,
(4) derive RH conditionally.
Competing operator/resolvent formulations are demoted to secondary remarks or notes.

\paragraph{What this revision does not claim.}
This revision does not claim that the choice of $\Phi$ is uniquely justified.
It does not claim that Target~A or Target~B is proved.
It does not claim RH.

\paragraph{Research posture.}
The manuscript remains a proposed proof framework under review.
The argument attempts to show that this single-object route can support a valid RH reduction once the open bridges are discharged.
